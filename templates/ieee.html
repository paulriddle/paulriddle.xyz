Why? Why use IEEE instead of simply using binary fractions: \[ b_{m}b_{m-1} \cdots b_{1}b_{0} \centerdot b_{-1}b_{-2} \cdots b_{-n+1}b_{-n} \] \[ b = \sum_{i=-m}^{m}2^i \times b_i \] Because to represent a big number like DBL_MAX = 1.7976931348623157E+308 would take a fuckload of digits. I don't know know how many digits that is. But with IEEE it's just 64 digits. I'm sure there are other reasons but I don't care about them and this one does not impress me either. Basically you have smaller range of representable values if you use the simple method of binary fractions. And as such it is unsuitable.

Numbers of the binary form 0.1111...111 are just below zero. Example (6 digits): \[ \begin{aligned} 0.111111 &= 2^{-1} + 2^{-2} + 2^{-3} + 2^{-3} + 2^{-4} + 2^{-5} + 2^{-6} \\ &= {1 \over 2^1} + {1 \over 2^2} + {1 \over 2^3} + {1 \over 2^4} + {1 \over 2^5} + {1 \over 2^6} \\ &= \tfrac{32}{64} + \tfrac{16}{64} + \tfrac{8}{64} + \tfrac{4}{64} + \tfrac{2}{64} + \tfrac{1}{64} \\ &= \tfrac{63}{64} \end{aligned} \]
